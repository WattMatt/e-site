Executive Summary

This report apportions the capital and operating cost of the standby generation installed at Sandton Gate Mall across its tenants on a fair, defensible and fully auditable basis. The installation comprises three diesel generator sets providing N+1 cover to the centre's essential load. Capital is recovered over a ten-year term, while diesel and maintenance are recovered through a per-kWh operational tariff. The resulting monthly recovery reconciles exactly to the amortised capital repayment, with every tenant — including those not on the generator — shown explicitly.

Methodology & Load Provision

Each tenant's standby demand is derived from its lettable area and trade category, using category load densities consistent with SANS 10142 diversified-demand practice, and overridden with metered or specified figures where these are available. Loads are summed per zone to confirm that connected demand sits within the firm rating of the generators serving that zone. Tenants on their own generation, or electing not to take standby supply, contribute zero load to the shared pool and are excluded from cost recovery while remaining on the schedule for transparency.

Cost Structure

Capital cost of R3.0 million covers the generator sets, tenant and main board modifications, sub-main and reticulation cabling, and the control, metering and BMS tie-in. This principal is amortised over 120 months at 1.00% per month, giving a fixed monthly capital repayment that forms the recovery target. Running cost is treated separately as a usage-based tariff of R7.98/kWh, built up from the prevailing diesel price and a maintenance provision, with a 10% contingency applied.

Tenant Allocation Approach

The fixed monthly capital repayment is shared between participating tenants in direct proportion to each tenant's standby load, so that a tenant drawing twice the demand carries twice the capital share. Allocations are computed to the nearest Rand and the final participating tenant absorbs any rounding residue, guaranteeing that the sum of tenant charges equals the capital repayment to the cent. The per-kWh operational tariff is billed separately against each tenant's actual standby consumption.

Recommendations

We recommend that standby metering be commissioned per tenant so that operational tariff billing reflects measured consumption rather than estimates, and that the capital schedule be reviewed annually against actual finance cost. Tenants currently on own generation should be re-canvassed at lease renewal, as consolidating them onto the centre installation would lower the unit cost borne by all participating tenants.
